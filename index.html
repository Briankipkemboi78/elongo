EPI elongo pastrolist initiative

Introducing modern agriculture to pastoralist communities. Main purpose is to transform smallholder farmers in pastoral communities into modern farmers to increase crop and livestock productivity in terms of profitability and resilience to ensure food security. Together, we can help pastrolists reduce poverty, give access to education, provide ways to conserve water, and support environmental conservation.

About Us

Elongo Pastrolists Initiative was founded in XXXX. We are dedicated to supporting pastrolists and fostering sustainable livelihoods for communities that rely on livestock. We understand the unique challenges faced by pastrolists and strive to empower them with knowledge,resources, and opportunities needed to thrive in ever-changing world.

Our Mission

Our mission is to enhance the resilience and well-being of pastoralist communities by promoting sustainable practices, facilitating access to essential services, and advocating for their rights. We believe in preserving and celebrating the rich cultural heritage and invaluable contributions of pastoralists to our global society.

What We Do

  • We offer Scholarships and learning materials to pastoral children in school.
  • We build capacity of youth and women on enterpreneurship skills like beadwork, leather work and bee keeping.
  • As an organization, we strive to improve access to water through drilling of boreholes, construction of water pans/dams as well as water harvesting.
  • We Advocate for environmental conservation in pastoral arears and plant trees.

Programs

Hay | Livestock Farming

Hay harvesting is the way forward to reduce pastoralists and loss of livestock during drought seasons. Several women groups have been awarded farm inputs that are used to harvest hay.

Meat Production

After 5 years achievement in Meat value addition, various Slaughter house owners and stake holders, have undergone training on Meat Value Addition. According to the report by the Ministry of Livestock in 2020, Kajiado County has been identified as the best county in Kenya producing Natural Meat which is considered important for domestic as well as export markets

Milk Production

Commercial milk production have improved over the last 5 years. Currently, Dairy Cooperatives and as high as Kshs 200,000,000 a month in Kajiado county. Through this, women were able to promote Girl Child Education. This has reduced school cases of dropout Under the cooperative dairy Maasai Women are currently capable of promoting girl child education without school fees challenges.

Fish Farming

Its the way of embracing fish farming among the Maasai community who mostly rely on livestock production. Enkasiti Women Group are among the groups that have fish ponds from which they harvest fish for sale and to accruement on their food production.

Tomato Farming

Kajiado County has been identified as the highest in tomato production in Kenya. It's due to the department became supportive to the individual and women groups of tomato growers in Kajiado County. The region interms of agricultural belt produces tomatoes worth Ksh 1.3 Billions between 2019 and 2021, and due to that it has emerged Kajiado County to become the largests commercial of tomato producer in Kenya currently.
