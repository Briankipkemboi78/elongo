EPI Elongo Pastoralists Initiative

Introducing modern agriculture to pastoralist communities. Main purpose is to transform smallholder farmers in pastoral communities into modern farmers to increase crop and livestock productivity in terms of profitability and resilience to ensure food security. Together, we can help pastoralist reduce poverty, give access to education, provide ways to conserve water, and support environmental conservation.

About Us

EPI

The Elongo Pastoralists Initiative was founded in 2016 with the aim of supporting pastoralists and fostering sustainable livelihoods for communities dependent on livestock. We acknowledge the unique challenges faced by pastoralists and strive to offer them the necessary information, opportunities, and resources to thrive in an ever-evolving environment.

Our Mission

Our mission is to enhance the resilience and well-being of pastoralist communities by promoting sustainable practices, facilitating access to essential services, and advocating for their rights. We believe in preserving and celebrating the rich cultural heritage and invaluable contributions of pastoralists to our global society.

What We Do

A variety of initiatives are involved in helping pastoralists improve their standard of living. First and foremost, it is crucial to implement capacity-building and training programs that address issues like better methods for managing livestock, financial and business management, marketing tactics, and climate change adaptation. Second, it is important to make it easier for people to access resources like veterinary care, animal healthcare facilities, water resources by building or improving water points, grazing reserves, and credit and microfinance services. Thirdly, it's critical to diversify sources of income by promoting alternative sources of revenue and offering job training in industries other than cattle, such as agriculture, beekeeping, or handcraft manufacturing. Market development activities can assist group marketing initiatives, add value through processing and packaging facilities, and conduct market research. Infrastructure development includes enhancing the network of roads, storage spaces, and communications facilities. In order to defend the rights of pastoralists, advocacy is crucial.

Programs

EPI

Hay | Livestock Farming

Hay harvesting is the way forward to reduce pastoralists and loss of livestock during drought seasons. Several women groups have been awarded farm inputs that are used to harvest hay.

Meat Production

After 5 years achievement in Meat value addition, various Slaughter house owners and stake holders, have undergone training on Meat Value Addition. According to the report by the Ministry of Livestock in 2020, Kajiado County has been identified as the best county in Kenya producing Natural Meat which is considered important for domestic as well as export markets

Milk Production

Commercial milk production have improved over the last 5 years. Currently, Dairy Cooperatives and as high as Kshs 200,000,000 a month in Kajiado county. Through this, women were able to promote Girl Child Education. This has reduced school cases of dropout Under the cooperative dairy Maasai Women are currently capable of promoting girl child education without school fees challenges.

Fish Farming

Its the way of embracing fish farming among the Maasai community who mostly rely on livestock production. Enkasiti Women Group are among the groups that have fish ponds from which they harvest fish for sale and to accruement on their food production.

Tomato Farming

Kajiado County has been identified as the highest in tomato production in Kenya. It's due to the department became supportive to the individual and women groups of tomato growers in Kajiado County. The region interms of agricultural belt produces tomatoes worth Ksh 1.3 Billions between 2019 and 2021, and due to that it has emerged Kajiado County to become the largest commercial of tomato producer in Kenya currently.
